OIL SPILL

ACTION PLAN



Goal: To establish a comprehensive and improved oil spill response plan.

STRATEGIC STEPS

  1. Conduct a risk assessment to identify existing oil spill risks, high-risk areas, and potential oil spill sources. (2 months)

  2. Establish stricter and improved regulations by assessing and updating existing oil spill response regulations, and increasing penalties for violations and irresponsible practices. (4 months)

  3. Initiate a public awareness campaign about the importance of oil spill prevention. (2 months)

  4. Develop and implement a detailed national oil spill response plan with well-trained response teams and necessary equipment. (5 months)

  5. Train response teams in oil spill containment, cleanup, and marine life protection. (6 months)

  6. Early testing of the use of technology, by employing satellite monitoring, in detecting oil spill incidents (6 months)

  7. Develop educational programs to raise awareness among coastal communities about the risks of oil spills and encourage them to report and monitor potential oil spill incidents. (5 months)

  8. Invest in advanced and technological oil spill detection while creating a public database of oil spill incidents and sources. (6 months)

  9. Develop partnerships with research institutions and investors, and allocate funds for research on eco-friendly response technologies. (6 months)

  10. Continuously assess the effectiveness of the oil spill prevention and response measures.


    • Timeframe: 3 and a half years

WHAT CAN WE DO FOR NOW?

  1. Maintain and expand public awareness campaigns while also promoting the use of renewable energy and reducing fossil fuel reliance.

  2. Tighten regulations on oil-related industries.

  3. Employ techniques including oil-absorbent materials, skimmers, oil booms, bioremediation, vacuum pumps, manual and mechanical removal, high-pressure hot water, gelatin treatment, and in-situ burning.

PERSONS INVOLVED

    • Department of Environment and Natural Resources
    • DA - Bureau of Fisheries and Aquatic Resources
    • Philippine Coast Guard Operations Center (for oil & HNS)
    • Marine Environmental Protection Command (MEPCOM)
    • Fishermen, Tourists, Businessmen
    • Sea Nomads/Sea Gypsies (Badjao)
    • Coastal communities
